# 0.3.0 single-mode run — Sonnet 4.6, n=2

First benchmark of the single-mode capybaraa (0.3.0, conscious gate, `act='on'`). Small
and directional: three tasks, three arms, two runs each. Deterministic gates only (no
judge API). Reproduce:

```bash
cd benchmarks/agentic
for t in lean-native hygiene-replace feat-rating; do
  python3 run.py --task "$t" --arms baseline,capybaraa,ponytail --models sonnet --runs 2 --workers 3
done
```

## Results (median LOC; lower is leaner)

| Task | gate | baseline | capybaraa | ponytail |
|------|------|---------:|----------:|---------:|
| lean-native    | native used, correct, safe | 1 | 1 | 1 |
| hygiene-replace| replaced not piled, correct | 23.5 | **16.0** | 9.0 |
| feat-rating    | built, correct (LOC tier)   | 46.0 | **25.5** | 17.5 |

All cells: correct% = 1.0, safe% = 1.0, gate% = 1.0. Tests written: 0 across every arm on
these three tasks. Cost/latency: capybaraa is slightly higher on both (e.g. feat-rating
$0.117 / 26s vs baseline $0.103 / 21s) — the price of clarifying and checking.

## Honest read

- **lean-native ties.** The answer is one line (`<input type="date">`); a strong model
  gets it with or without the plugin. No signal, kept in as the floor case.
- **capybaraa beats the bare baseline on over-build** by ~32% (hygiene-replace) to ~45%
  (feat-rating) without dropping correctness, safety, or the hygiene gate.
- **ponytail is leaner still** on raw LOC. These are line-count tasks, ponytail's home
  turf. Capybaraa's claim is not "fewest lines" — it is leanness without cutting safety
  or completeness, plus clarify-first behavior the LOC gates here don't capture.
- n=2, one model, three tasks. Treat as directional, not a verdict. The clarify and
  tests-written dimensions need the judge run (skipped here to stay cheap); the earlier
  five-arm Haiku writeup covers them.
